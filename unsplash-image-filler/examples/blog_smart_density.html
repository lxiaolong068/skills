Across the Coastline

The morning air was crisp and salty as the train pulled into the coastal town.

I walked along the harbor, watching fishermen mend nets and gulls circle overhead.

The local market was already busy, with crates of citrus and baskets of herbs piled high.

I stopped for coffee and listened to stories about storms that reshaped the shoreline.

By noon, the light turned golden and the cliffs glowed above the beach.

A narrow trail climbed through pines, revealing a stretch of hidden coves.

Each cove felt quieter than the last, with only the sound of waves and wind.

I paused to sketch the waterline and the scattered stones on the sand.

Later, the town square filled with music and the scent of grilled seafood.

A vendor shared a recipe passed down through three generations of cooks.

Evening arrived quickly, and the harbor lights blinked on one by one.

I ended the day on the pier, watching the horizon darken into deep blue.
